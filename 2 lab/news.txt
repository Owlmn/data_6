Title: Pakistan bat against Sussex in warm up
Published: 7/8/2016

Summary:
Pakistan elected to bat first after Sussex won the toss in their second warm-up match. The team made three changes from their previous draw against Somerset, resting key bowlers Mohammad Amir, Rahat Ali, and Yasir Shah. The first warm-up had ended in a draw with centuries from Pakistan's Younis Khan and Azhar Ali.

Title: Griffith Hick named as Australia coaches for ODI seri
Published: 4/15/2016

Summary:
Adam Griffith and Graham Hick will temporarily join Australia's coaching staff for the one-day series in the West Indies in June. Justin Langer will lead the team to give head coach Darren Lehmann a break before the tour of Sri Lanka. The appointments come as Australia searches for a full-time bowling coach following Craig McDermott's departure.

Title: Oil prices edge up on anticipation of OPEC led production cu
Published: 11/23/2016

Summary:
Oil prices edged up slightly on Wednesday, with WTI crude at $46.13 and Brent at $49.22 per barrel, as traders anticipated an OPEC-led production cut expected to be finalized at a November 30 meeting. However, trading remained thin ahead of the U.S. Thanksgiving holiday, and analysts noted the market was reluctant to push prices above $50 a barrel. Concerns emerged after a preliminary OPEC meeting failed to resolve issues around Iran and Iraq's willingness to participate in the production cut, though analysts still expected a deal to be reached next week.

Title: New Zealand usher in new era against Zimbab
Published: 7/27/2016

Summary:
New Zealand's new Test captain Kane Williamson has vowed to continue the team's positive, attacking approach under his leadership, though he acknowledges adapting tactics may be necessary for the conditions in Zimbabwe. He takes over from Brendon McCullum, who transformed the team's image and led them to the 50-over World Cup final. New Zealand is expecting a tough challenge from Zimbabwe, who are returning to Test cricket after a long absence, and recall a close match in Bulawayo in 2011.

Title: oil climbs for 5th day as us output edges lower
Published: 4/16/2015

Summary:
US oil prices surged to a 2015 high after a slight dip in domestic production fueled speculation that the oversupplied market might be tightening. However, analysts cautioned the rally may be premature, noting that US inventories remain at multi-decade highs. The International Energy Agency also revised its forecasts, cutting non-OPEC supply growth while increasing its global demand outlook for 2015.
